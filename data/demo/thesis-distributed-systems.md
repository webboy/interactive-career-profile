# Thesis Excerpt — Reliable Event Processing in Distributed Systems

Author: Alex Rivera
Program: M.Sc. Computer Science (Distributed Systems)

## Abstract

This work evaluates backpressure strategies for event-driven microservice architectures. Experiments compare queue sizing policies, idempotent consumer design, and observability signals that predict cascading failures.

## Selected findings

- Idempotent handlers reduced duplicate-processing incidents by 87% in synthetic load tests.
- Adaptive batching improved throughput without sacrificing p95 latency targets.
- Structured tracing correlated strongly with faster root-cause analysis during failure injection.

## Relevance

The research complements Alex's platform engineering experience with evidence of systems thinking, experimentation, and operational reliability practices.
